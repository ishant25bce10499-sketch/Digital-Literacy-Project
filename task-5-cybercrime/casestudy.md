Case Study: Ransomware Attack on a College Student
Ransomware is a type of cybercrime in which malicious software encrypts a victim’s files or locks their system, and the attacker demands payment (ransom) to restore access.
In this scenario, a college student named Arjun downloads a free version of a popular software from an unverified website. The step-by-step attack occurs as follows:
1.	Arjun installs the downloaded file, which secretly contains ransomware. 
2.	Once installed, the malware runs in the background without his knowledge. 
3.	It quickly encrypts important files such as assignments, photos, and project data. 
4.	A message appears on his screen stating that his files are locked and can only be recovered by paying a ransom in cryptocurrency. 
5.	The attacker threatens to permanently delete the files if payment is not made within a limited time. 
Ransomware attacks commonly target students, small businesses, and organizations that may lack strong cybersecurity measures or regularly updated backups.
The consequences can be serious. Victims may lose access to critical academic work, personal files, and sensitive data. Paying the ransom does not guarantee file recovery, and it may encourage further attacks. In Arjun’s case, he lost his semester project because he had no backup, which affected his academic performance. Additionally, the incident caused stress and financial loss.
This case highlights the importance of downloading software only from trusted sources, regularly backing up data, and using updated antivirus protection to prevent such attacks.
