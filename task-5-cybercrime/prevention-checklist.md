Stay Safe Online – Checklist for College Students (India)

✅ Essential Safety Tips
Use strong, unique passwords for each account and enable two-factor authentication (2FA).
Avoid clicking on suspicious links in emails, SMS, or social media messages.
Download apps/software only from official sources like Google Play Store or Apple App Store.
Keep your devices updated with the latest security patches and antivirus software.
Limit personal information sharing (address, phone number, ID details) on public platforms.
Log out from shared/public computers and avoid saving passwords on them.
Verify profiles before interacting—fake accounts are common in scams and phishing.
Regularly back up important data (assignments, documents) to cloud or external storage.

💸 UPI & Financial Safety Tips
Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or bank details with anyone—even if they claim to be from your bank.
Always check payment requests carefully—remember, receiving money does NOT require entering your UPI PIN.
Use only trusted apps like Google Pay, PhonePe, or Paytm.
Beware of “scan & receive money” scams—QR codes are usually for making payments, not receiving them.

🚨 If Something Goes Wrong
Immediately report financial fraud by calling Helpline 1930.
File a complaint on the National Cyber Crime Portal: National Cyber Crime Portal.
Inform your bank and block your account if you suspect unauthorized transactions.
